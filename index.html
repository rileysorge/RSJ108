  1. Figure out a reliable source for your topic

    For example: If your story is on the rise of a new teaching method in elementary schools, you could interview an elementary school teacher and an early education professor.

  2. Contact your source and confirm an interview time.

    Once you've obtained your source, send them an email or give them a call to confirm a time and location to conduct the interview.

  3. Prepare your thoughtful questions

    Start brainstorming tailored questions to each interview subject. Start with your "who, what, when, where, why, how?" Then think of how each source can give a different perspective based on their background.
